iOS accessibility app that helps low-vision users read and navigate on device with clarity and speed

Overview

Seeing Reality is a Swift and SwiftUI app designed for people with visual impairments. It combines zoom functionality, on-device OCR, and high quality text to speech to make printed and on screen content easier to read. I led the design and iOS build with a focus on low cognitive load, large tap targets, and VoiceOver compatibility.

What I built

  • SwiftUI interface with single purpose screens, oversized controls, and clear visual hierarchy
  • Live magnifier with pinch and tap zoom, torch control, and contrast presets for common reading scenarios
  • On-device OCR using Vision to capture text, then AVFoundation to read it aloud with adjustable rate and voice
  • Offline first workflow that keeps OCR and speech functional without a network connection
  • VoiceOver labels, Dynamic Type, and color contrast checks aligned with Apple HIG and WCAG guidance

Tech stack

  • SwiftUI for layout, state, and adaptive UI
  • Vision for OCR text detection and recognition
  • AVFoundation for speech synthesis and audio session control
  • UIKit bridges where needed for camera capture configuration
  • Accessibility APIs for VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, and reduce motion settings

Key features

  • Large buttons, high contrast, generous spacing for low-vision use
  • Text to Speech, on-device OCR, quick repeat
  • Navigation with spoken directions, haptics for confirmations, simple cancel
  • Works offline for OCR and speech, respects Dynamic Type and reduce motion

Outcomes

  • Prototype tested with educators and low-vision users to validate control size and reading flow
  • Improved legibility and speed to first audio playback through simplified capture and read flow
  • Documented patterns for future accessibility projects across magnification, OCR, and speech
